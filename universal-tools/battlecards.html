Discovery Questions
▶
Question 1
"What percentage of your Citrix environment actually runs in Azure, and how much remains on-premises?"
Why it works: Citrix's strength is hybrid, but this often masks the complexity and cost of maintaining two architectures. Prospects typically admit most workloads are still on-prem.
Expected response: "About 30-40% in Azure, rest on-premises" or "We're still evaluating cloud migration."
Follow-up: "What's preventing the rest from moving? Is it cost, complexity, or capability concerns?" This opens the door to Nerdio's cloud-native simplicity.
Question 2
"How are you currently optimizing Azure costs for your Citrix VMs—auto-scaling, spot instances, scheduled shutdowns?"
Why it works: Citrix requires manual setup or third-party tools. Most organizations aren't doing this well, leaving 20-40% of spend on the table.
Expected response: "We use scheduled shutdowns" or "We're not doing much yet" or "We've looked at auto-scaling but it's complicated."
Follow-up: "How much do you estimate that's costing you annually?" Once quantified, position Nerdio's intelligent, automatic cost optimization.
Question 3
"When a Citrix component fails—a broker, session host, or licensing server—how long until your users are back online?"
Why it works: Citrix's complexity means long MTTR (mean time to recovery). Most organizations have no true self-healing; they rely on reactive monitoring and manual fixes.
Expected response: "Depends on what failed, usually 30 minutes to 2 hours" or "We have a dedicated team watching it."
Follow-up: "Nerdio's self-healing engine proactively detects and repairs 80+ failure modes automatically. Would that reduce your on-call burden?"
Question 4
"How is Citrix currently integrated with Intune—are you managing policies, patches, and app distribution natively, or through workarounds?"
Why it works: Citrix/Intune integration is notoriously weak. Most orgs use Citrix as a silo, missing out on unified endpoint management.
Expected response: "It's complicated" or "We manage Citrix separately from Intune" or "We're not leveraging Intune as much as we could."
Follow-up: "Nerdio is Intune-first, so policies, compliance, and app management flow naturally. What would that mean for your IT team's efficiency?"
Question 5
"If you were to migrate off Citrix tomorrow, how long would it take, and what would be your top three blockers?"
Why it works: This surfaces migration anxiety. Citrix lock-in is real, and the prospect may be frustrated with it.
Expected response: "Months... it's complicated" or "We'd need to rebuild from scratch" or "Custom apps, legacy dependencies."
Follow-up: "Nerdio simplifies this. Would a faster, less risky path to modernization be valuable?"
Question 6
"What is your actual cost per user per month for Citrix (licensing, infrastructure, management)?"
Why it works: Most Citrix customers have no idea—the cost is fragmented across licenses, infrastructure, and hidden overhead.
Expected response: "Umm... I'd have to calculate that" or "Somewhere between $50-150/user."
Follow-up: Position Nerdio's transparent cost model and typical 30-50% savings after optimization.
