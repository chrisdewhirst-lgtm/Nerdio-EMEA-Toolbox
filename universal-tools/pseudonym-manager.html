ℹ️ About This Data

Session Storage: All mappings are stored in your browser's memory only. They will be lost when you close this tab or browser unless you export them.

Sensitive Data: Your mapping file contains real customer information paired with pseudonyms. Treat it like customer data—don't share externally without approval.

Recommended Practice: Export your workspace after completing a deal, then import it the next time you work with that account to maintain consistency.

The Pseudonym Workflow
▼

Step 1: Before Using External AI Tools

Open the Pseudonym Manager and create or select your workspace for this deal.

Step 2: Map All Sensitive Data

Add every company name, contact person, email, deal value, and technology reference to your mapping table. Be thorough—if it could identify the customer, it needs a pseudonym.

Step 3: Pseudonymise Your Prompt

Paste your original prompt or content into the "Paste Original Text" field, then click "Pseudonymise →". The tool will scan for all mapped terms and swap them automatically.

Step 4: Work in External AI

Copy the clean (pseudonymised) text and paste it into Claude, ChatGPT, or another external AI tool. You now have zero real customer data exposed.

Step 5: Get Your Output

Let the AI work its magic. Copy the AI-generated content when you're satisfied.

Step 6: De-pseudonymise in Approved Tools

Use the "De-pseudonymise Text" tab to preview the swap. Then paste the de-pseudonymised output into Microsoft Copilot or your approved internal system for the final, real version.

Step 7: Review & Finalize

Always double-check that all pseudonyms were caught and all real data was properly restored. Look for partial matches or abbreviations the tool might have missed.

The Rules
▼

Core Rules

  • NEVER paste real customer names, deal values, or contact details into external AI tools
  • ALWAYS use the Pseudonym Manager before working with customer-specific content
  • The mapping file is sensitive—treat it like customer data (don't share externally)
  • When in doubt, pseudonymise MORE, not less
  • Partial matches matter—"Contoso" might appear as "the Contoso team"—check for variants
  • Review every output before it leaves your screen

Key Principle

The pseudonym workflow ensures your real customer data never touches external AI systems. If you slip up and include real data, the mapping file stays on your machine. By the time anything leaves your control, it's pseudonymised.

What MUST Be Pseudonymised
▼
  • Company names and subsidiaries
  • Individual names and titles
  • Email addresses and phone numbers
  • Specific deal values and contract terms
  • Internal project codenames
  • Specific technology stack details (if confidential)
  • Location details that could identify the customer (street addresses, branch offices)
What's Usually SAFE to Keep
▼
  • Industry vertical (Healthcare, Financial Services, etc.)
  • Company size range (500–1,000 employees)
  • General geography (US, EMEA, APAC)
  • Generic technology categories (VDI, cloud desktop, endpoint management)
  • Nerdio product names (NME, NMM—these are public)
Using Microsoft Copilot for De-pseudonymisation
▼

Why Copilot? Microsoft Copilot (or your organisation's approved internal AI tooling) operates within your company's data boundary. Customer data stays inside your Microsoft 365 tenant. This makes it the safe place to perform the final swap from pseudonyms back to real names.

Step-by-Step: De-pseudonymising in Copilot

1. Export your mapping table

In the Pseudonym Manager, go to the Export tab and click "Copy as Table". This copies your pseudonym ↔ real value mapping in a clean format Copilot can understand.

2. Open Microsoft Copilot

Open Copilot in Word, Teams, or the standalone Copilot chat — whichever is approved in your organisation. If using Copilot in Word, open a new document first.

3. Give Copilot the mapping and the text

Use a prompt like this (copy and adapt):

Prompt for Copilot:

I have a document that uses placeholder names (pseudonyms) instead of real customer data. Please replace every pseudonym with the corresponding real value using the mapping below. Do not change anything else in the text — only swap the exact terms listed.

MAPPING TABLE:
[Paste your exported mapping table here]

TEXT TO DE-PSEUDONYMISE:
[Paste the AI-generated output containing pseudonyms here]

Please return the full text with all pseudonyms replaced. Flag any pseudonyms you couldn't match.

4. Review the output

Check Copilot's output carefully:

  • Were ALL pseudonyms replaced? (Search for any remaining Greek-alphabet names or fake names)
  • Were replacements accurate? (Correct real value for each pseudonym)
  • Did Copilot change anything else? (It shouldn't — but always verify)

5. Finalise in your approved system

Once verified, the document now contains real customer data and should be handled according to your organisation's data policies — save to approved locations, share only with authorised colleagues.

Alternative Copilot Methods

Copilot in Word (best for long documents):

  • Paste the pseudonymised AI output into a Word document
  • Use Copilot's "Rewrite" or chat sidebar to request the swap
  • Copilot can process the whole document in context
  • Use Track Changes so you can see exactly what was modified

Copilot in Excel (best for structured data / bulk swaps):

  • Put your mapping table in Sheet 1 (Column A = Pseudonym, Column B = Real Value)
  • Put your content in Sheet 2
  • Ask Copilot to create SUBSTITUTE formulas to perform all replacements
  • This is particularly useful for bulk operations — e.g., swapping names across 50 emails

Find & Replace in Word (simplest — no AI needed):

  • If you only have 5-10 pseudonyms, a simple Ctrl+H Find & Replace is fastest
  • Work through your mapping list one by one
  • Use "Replace All" for each pseudonym → real value pair
  • This keeps everything inside approved Microsoft tooling with zero AI involvement

What NOT to Do

  • DON'T paste the mapping table into Claude, ChatGPT, or any external AI tool — the mapping IS the sensitive data
  • DON'T de-pseudonymise in a tool your organisation hasn't approved for customer data
  • DON'T skip the review step — always verify every swap was correct before sharing
  • DON'T store the mapping file in an external or shared location — keep it local or in approved internal storage
Approved vs. External Tools — Quick Reference
▼

✅ Approved (Real Data OK)

  • Microsoft Copilot (Word, Excel, Teams, standalone)
  • Microsoft 365 apps (Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook)
  • SharePoint / OneDrive (company tenant)
  • Your CRM (Salesforce, HubSpot, Dynamics)
  • Internal communication tools (Teams, company Slack)
  • Any tool approved by your IT/Security team

Check with your IT team — approved tools may vary by organisation.

🚫 External (Pseudonyms Only)

  • Claude (Anthropic)
  • ChatGPT (OpenAI)
  • Gemini (Google — consumer version)
  • Perplexity, Grok, or other public AI tools
  • Any AI tool not covered by your company's data agreement
  • Personal email, personal cloud storage

These tools are powerful — use them freely, but only with pseudonymised data.

Example Workflow
▼

Scenario: Creating an Account Plan for a Bank

Original Text (UNSAFE for external AI):

"Sarah Mitchell at Woodgrove Bank is evaluating Nerdio NME to replace their Citrix Virtual Apps 2203 deployment. The deal is worth $234K ARR. David Chen (IT Director) is the technical champion."

Mapped Pseudonyms:

  • Woodgrove Bank → Alpha Financial Corp
  • Sarah Mitchell → Jordan Blake
  • David Chen → Morgan Chase
  • Citrix Virtual Apps 2203 → Legacy VDI Platform A
  • $234K ARR → $200K–250K range

Pseudonymised Text (SAFE for Claude/ChatGPT):

"Jordan Blake at Alpha Financial Corp is evaluating Nerdio NME to replace their Legacy VDI Platform A deployment. The deal is in the $200K–250K range. Morgan Chase (IT Director) is the technical champion."

AI Output (from ChatGPT):

"Account Plan for Alpha Financial Corp: Position Nerdio NME as a cost-effective replacement for Legacy VDI Platform A. Key stakeholders: Jordan Blake (executive sponsor) and Morgan Chase (technical lead). Estimated contract value: $200K–250K."

De-pseudonymised Output (for final approval system):

"Account Plan for Woodgrove Bank: Position Nerdio NME as a cost-effective replacement for Citrix Virtual Apps 2203. Key stakeholders: Sarah Mitchell (executive sponsor) and David Chen (technical lead). Estimated contract value: $234K ARR."

Now you can paste this into Microsoft Copilot or your internal system with full confidence that the real customer data is correct.
